Portcentere - Havencentrum Antwerpen

European Project Semester

FEB'17 JUL'17

The Port Centre is a visitors and information centre to the port, the industry and the logistics in Antwerp. Each year almost 50.000 visitors live a unique port experience by participating to one of the guided visits in the port area and the interactive exhibition. Thereby the Port Centre can rely on 85 port guides.

From February 2017 until July 2017 I worked in cooperation with the Port Centre to create a concept for their new Port Tour. My role in this project was as the Team Leader.

Goal of the Project:

Create a gamified solution to make the port bus tour more enticing by using interesting facts, thrilling technologies and an overwhelming range of transactions. It needs to improve retention, involvement and impact of a guided tour along the port.

Original Brief

The game had to have one theme, an introduction and a storyline as well as being modular. There had to be a safety aspect as students maybe walking around the port and they shouldn’t be distracted. It needed to cater for at least 50 people and adopt a less is more approach.

Target Group

For this project the target group were 15-18 year olds, they are bored on the tour, unlike younger people they’re unengaged and impatient. Hence their attention span is small and they wanted freedom as they have too much energy this results in them having little respect for authority.

European Project Semester

Eu­ro­pean Project Se­mes­ter is a pro­gramme of­fered by 18 Eu­ro­pean uni­ver­si­ties in 12 coun­tries through­out Eu­rope to stu­dents who have com­pleted at least two years of study. EPS is cre­ated with en­gi­neer­ing stu­dents in mind, but other stu­dents who can par­tic­i­pate in an en­gi­neer­ing project are also welcome. It is crafted to ad­dress the de­sign re­quire­ments of the de­gree and pre­pare en­gi­neer­ing stu­dents with all the nec­es­sary skills to face the chal­lenges of today’s world econ­omy. EPS is a mix­ture of “Project Re­lated Courses” and project organized/​problem based learn­ing. Stu­dents work in in­ter­na­tional and usu­ally in­ter­dis­ci­pli­nary teams of 3–6 stu­dents on their projects, and most of the projects are in­ter­dis­ci­pli­nary. Some projects are done in co­op­er­a­tion with com­mer­cial busi­nesses and in­dus­tries, other projects are more aca­d­e­mic. Stu­dents learn to learn and to take re­spon­s­abil­ity for their learn­ing and their project work and they de­velop their in­ter­cul­tural com­pe­tences, their com­mu­ni­ca­tion skills and their in­ter­per­sonal skills.

You can find out more about the project at https://maltegrapentin.com/portcentre/
